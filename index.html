// 001 — About

Building chips
from scratch.

I'm a second-year Electronics Engineering student specialising in VLSI Design & Technology at R.M.K College of Engineering and Technology, Chennai.

My focus is the complete digital chip design pipeline — writing synthesisable RTL in Verilog, running functional verification with self-written testbenches, and pushing designs through synthesis, place & route, all the way to GDS II layout.

I work with Cadence Xcelium for simulation and Xilinx Vivado for FPGA prototyping — but most of my work lives inside a tool I built myself.

★ Flagship Project

Silis EDA Platform

Commercial EDA tools like Cadence and Synopsys are expensive and inaccessible for students. So I built Silis — a licensed open-source EDA platform that lets any student run the full RTL → GDS II chip design flow for free.

Adopted across multiple engineering colleges. Every project in my portfolio was designed, simulated, and taped out inside Silis.
